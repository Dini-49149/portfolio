Project Overview

In the quest for veracity, our Automated Lie Detection project stands at the forefront, blending the intricacies of psychological analysis with the precision of deep learning. We have engineered a multifaceted AI system that meticulously examines video, audio, and textual cues to uncover the truth behind the words and gestures.

Central to our innovation are Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), which work in concert to parse through complex multimodal data. The CNNs delve into the visual domain, scrutinizing facial expressions and movements frame by frame, while the RNNs dissect the sequential patterns of speech and intonation, capturing the essence of temporal deception indicators.

Our comprehensive dataset, sourced from Miami University's Multimodal Understanding of Social Interactions 3D (MU3D), encompasses a rich tapestry of human interactions, providing the diversity needed for an unbiased AI. By processing this data, we have significantly improved lie detection accuracy, transcending the capabilities of traditional polygraph methods.

This project is not just a technical achievement; it's a step towards safeguarding trust in an era where misinformation can have unprecedented consequences. From enhancing security protocols to supporting judicial processes, our system offers a beacon of reliability, promising a future where the truth can be ascertained with confidence.
